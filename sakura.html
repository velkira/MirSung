Sakura Sushi & Grill

About the Project

I asked my previous employer if I can use their assets to design a potential website. I followed a general guide of UX guidelines from start to finish. Since I no longer work there, I asked the owner and those around me of what they look for in a restaurant website. There were three main ones: menu, phone number and hours. I created user personas from local employees visiting for lunch to tourists sightseeing.

The Problem

Due to many trends of trying to have most of the information avaialble on a single page, I tried to incorprate that design into the website. There would be a scroll down function so users can either use the navigation bar to automatically scroll down to their preference, or simply scroll down using their smartphone or mouse. The restaurant’s menu was enormous so it was hard to add onto the main page without giving users information overload. However, due to a fixed navigation bar, having the menu on the main page could also work.

The Process

I also thought about what would a small business owner would need a website when there are websites like Yelp and Zomato? From my own personal experience and others, there were many websites where menu was not available or outdated. Hours were also ambiguous especially during the holidays. In addition, due to the restaurant being a popular location from many tourists going to the Rocky Mountains, it would be frustrating relying on other websites for hours and the menu where they may not be accurate due to the restaurant’s menu updates. So, I believed that the restaurant having its own website is beneficial due to those reasons.

The Process Cont.

I had to keep in mind the wants of the owner as well. The restaurant did not have a system to take in online orders. So, I did not make a page for online orders. The owner also did not want to spend money besides the possibility of keeping the website online so having imbedded Google Maps was not an option. However, due to the world being more digital and there are a lot of shy people, I believe online orders should be created in the restaurant.
